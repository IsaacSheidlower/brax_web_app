Tufts University - Task Description and Controls

Virtual Agents learning from human interaction

Below is a description of the task you will be asked to complete for this study:

What is the task?

PendulumBot is learning to balance. Your job is to give the robot feedback on how it’s doing, using the buttons at the bottom of the screen. If the PendulumBot falls or has balanced for 60 seconds, it will reset to its start position and try again. Feedback can be provided either “good” or “bad” feedback using the buttons on the bottom of the screen. “Good” feedback should be provided when PendulumBot is balancing or trying to balance near its vertical center. “Bad” feedback should be provided when the pendulum appears to be falling on its side.

PedulumBot Teaching Demo

What are the teaching controls?

You can teach PendulumBot via the “good” and “bad” feedback buttons. Use the mouse to click on the button you would like to select. When a button is selected, it will have a darker color and glow. When “good” feedback is provided, PendulumBot will become more likely to repeat its actions. When "bad" feedback is provided, PendulumBot will try not to repeat the same behavior. The more you press either button the more feedback PendulumBot will recieve. Feel free to provide as much feedback as you like.

Succesfuly completing the task.

The goal of the task is to teach PendulumBot to balance and prevent it from falling. You can teach the PendelumBot by providing "good" and "bad" feedback. If you successfully teach the PendulumBot, PendulumBot will not fall over and will continue to balance. Please continue to focus on the task for the remainder of the study.

Duration

The interaction will last for approximately 15 minutes (at most 16 minutes). After 15 minutes the pendulum window will close and you will be prompted to complete the study.
